Climate Analysis

Throughout history, the Earth’s climate has changed. In the last 650,000 years, there were several cycles of glacial advance and retreat. There are changes based on the Earth’s orbit. Current technology advances and satellites have allowed scientists to understand what is happening with the Earth. The current trend is very likely due to the result of human activity since the mid-20th century and is accelerating at a rate unprecedented.

Carbon dioxide and other gases traps heat. The greenhouse gases causes the Earth to warm. There are various evidences for the rapid climate change. Since the 19th century, the average surface temperature has increased by 1.62 degrees Fahrenheit.

Warming Oceans have increased by 0.4 degrees Fahrenheit since 1969. Ice sheets have also decreased with Antartica loosing ice mass about three times in the last decade. Also glaciers are retreating everywhere in the world. Snow cover in the Northern Hemisphere has also decreased in the past five decades with proof from the satellites. Global sea level rose about 8 inches in the last century and is increasing the rate every year. There has been a declining Arctic sea ice. This can also be witnessed with the number of high temperature events in the United States. There have been more extreme events. And finally another proof, is the acidity of surface ocean waters has increased.

Source: https://climate.nasa.gov/evidence/
